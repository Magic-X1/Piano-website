Music should be fun before it's perfect.

Technique matters, but it's not the point. The point is a kid who asks to practice instead of being told to, an adult who finally plays the song they always wanted to. Especially for beginners and younger students, I build lessons around pieces they actually want to play and small wins they can feel — because a student who loves music will keep playing long after the lesson ends, and that love is worth more than any drill.

About your teacher

Trained in the tradition. Taught with patience.

I'm Michael Xia, a classically trained pianist with 11 years of extensive experience behind the keys — including competition stages across Texas, with a 1st place finish in Region 23 and 2nd place at the Texas All-State piano competition. I teach students of every age — curious beginners, returning adults, and anyone working toward an exam or a recital — with an approach built on real technique, real theory, and a lot of listening.

Lessons happen in Katy, Texas and over video call, so distance is never the reason someone doesn't get to learn piano.

How lessons work

One method, three tempos.

Every student moves at a different pace. Lessons are built around where you're starting from, not a fixed curriculum.

Andante
Complete beginners, all ages

Posture, hand shape, and note reading — taught through pieces you'll actually enjoy playing, not just drills. The goal is a student who wants to sit down and play.

  • Fun, engaging pieces from day one
  • Reading music without the boredom
  • Ear training basics
Moderato
Returning & intermediate players

Rebuilding technique, tackling harder repertoire, and finally understanding the theory behind the notes.

  • Scales, chords & sight-reading
  • Repertoire from Bach to Debussy
  • Practice strategies that work
Con brio
Advanced students & exam prep

Polishing performance pieces, working toward grade exams, and developing real musical interpretation.

  • Exam & recital preparation
  • Advanced technique & interpretation
  • Performance coaching
